Balanced mix

MMM estimates what changed sales, then helps decide where the next budget should go.

It looks at historical sales, marketing spend, and outside factors over time. The model separates base demand from extra sales caused by media, then compares channels by sales impact, ROI, saturation, and next-dollar return.

1 Collect the data

Bring together weekly sales, channel spend, price, promotions, seasonality, and competitor signals.

2 Explain what happened

Estimate how much sales came from baseline demand, outside factors, and each media channel.

3 Choose the next move

Compare budget options and pick the mix with the best business trade-off.

Base demand

Sales you may get without this week's media, driven by brand, distribution, habit, seasonality, price, and market conditions.

Controls

Non-media factors such as price, holidays, competitor activity, weather, and promotions. These stop media taking credit for everything.

Media impact

The estimated sales caused by a channel after accounting for baseline demand and outside factors.

Saturation

When a channel still works, but each extra dollar creates less lift than the previous dollar.
